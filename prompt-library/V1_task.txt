## Task

Determine whether any text in the image was digitally altered after recapture. Evaluate visual signals only.

## Reasoning Approach

Reason through the visual evidence before concluding.

1. First scan the overall document layout and establish a typography baseline.
2. Then examine each text region for deviations.
3. If a signal is ambiguous or could be explained by document quality rather than tampering, flag it as low severity.
4. Do not infer tampering from a single weak signal alone.

## Signals to Evaluate

Look only for post-recapture digital insertion or modification:

- Text regions with inconsistent font weight, sharpness, or resolution relative to surrounding text
- Font size mismatches relative to surrounding text
- Mismatched shadows, edges, or backgrounds isolated to specific text areas
- Color or contrast discontinuities around specific characters or numbers
- Numbers or words that appear typographically inconsistent with surrounding prose (e.g., missing spaces, different baseline, font mismatch)

## Output Format

Return ONLY a JSON block with no preamble or explanation outside it:

{
  "verdict": "tampered | authentic | inconclusive",
  "confidence": "high | medium | low",
  "reasoning": "<summary of your step-by-step visual analysis>",
  "signals_detected": [
    {
      "signal_type": "<font_weight_inconsistency | resolution_mismatch | baseline_mismatch | shadow_edge_anomaly | color_contrast_discontinuity | font_size_mismatch | other>",
      "location": "<description of where in the document>",
      "severity": "high | medium | low"
    }
  ],
  "notes": "<optional caveats or ambiguities>"
}

If no signals are detected, return an empty array for signals_detected.
